Electrical Safety Tips

Follow these important safety guidelines to protect yourself, your family, and your property from electrical hazards. Safety should always be your #1 priority when electricity is involved.

At Home Safety

  • Keep emergency numbers near your phone
  • Install smoke detectors and test them monthly
  • Check plugs and cords regularly for damage
  • Store electrical components out of reach of children
  • Always protect electrical equipment from moisture
  • Do not use wet hands on cables or electrical devices
  • Follow electricity rules and regulations
  • Never overload electrical outlets
  • Use childproof covers on unused outlets
  • Keep electrical cords away from heat sources

Outdoor Electrical Safety

  • Keep electrical tools away from water
  • Never climb power poles or towers
  • Look up for power lines before using ladders
  • Keep kites and drones away from power lines
  • Use only weatherproof electrical products outdoors
  • Call before digging to avoid underground cables

Online Safety

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account
  • Always verify you are on the correct website
  • Be cautious when clicking on links or pop-ups
  • Never share personal information on unsecured sites
  • Keep your software and antivirus updated
  • Use two-factor authentication when available
  • Never touch electrical appliances with wet hands
  • Unplug appliances when not in use
  • Check cords for damage regularly
  • Install smoke detectors in every room
  • Keep electrical devices away from water
  • Replace damaged cords immediately
  • Turn off main power immediately
  • Call emergency services: 0800 123 456
  • Do not touch electrical fires with water
  • Use a Class C fire extinguisher
  • Never touch someone who has been shocked
  • Call a licensed electrician right away

General Electrical Rules to Follow

  • Never DIY major electrical work - Always hire a licensed electrician
  • Unplug appliances when not in use - Saves energy and prevents fires
  • Keep electrical devices away from water - Water and electricity don't mix
  • Replace damaged cords immediately - Frayed wires are dangerous
  • Don't pull the plug by the cord - Pull the plug itself
  • Use extension cords temporarily only - Not for permanent use
  • Keep space heaters 3 feet from anything flammable
  • Schedule professional electrical inspections every 5 years

WARNING: Electrical work can be dangerous and deadly. Always hire a licensed, qualified electrician for any electrical repairs, installations, or inspections.
